## What It Is

Deep work is sustained, distraction-free concentration on a cognitively demanding task. It produces work at a rate and quality that shallow work — interrupt-driven, low-cognitive-load activity — cannot match.

A *deep work practice* is the deliberate scheduling and protection of blocks of time in which deep work happens. The skill is not the focus itself, but building a life that makes the focus possible.

## When It Applies

- Producing original output that requires holding multiple constraints in working memory (writing, design, complex coding, hard analysis)
- Learning new skills at the edge of your current capability
- Any work where the marginal value of an extra hour of focus is meaningfully higher than the marginal value of an extra hour of shallow throughput

It does **not** apply to logistical work (calendar, routine email, status updates) — those are shallow by nature, and trying to do them "deeply" wastes the protected blocks.

## Core Mechanics

1. **Schedule the depth, not the shallow.** Block deep-work hours first in the day's plan; let meetings and email fill the rest.
2. **Pick a scheduling philosophy** that matches your life constraints:
   - **Monastic** — long retreats, no shallow obligations (rare; high output)
   - **Bimodal** — alternating weeks or days between depth and the rest of life
   - **Rhythmic** — same fixed hours every day (most sustainable for most people)
   - **Journalistic** — grab any free pocket on short notice (highest skill ceiling)
3. **Ritualise the conditions.** Fixed time, fixed place, fixed start cue. Removes decision cost and accelerates state entry.
4. **Drain shallow work to its minimum.** Audit each shallow task for the value it actually produces; eliminate or batch the rest.
5. **Respect attention residue.** Don't switch tasks before finishing a chunk; cognitive cleanup lag is real and measurable.

## Failure Modes

- **Calendar Tetris.** Booking back-to-back shallow meetings around fragments of "deep" time — the fragments are too short for actual depth.
- **Open-loop shallow.** Email and chat clients running during a deep block. Attention residue dominates; the block produces shallow-quality output disguised as depth.
- **Heroic philosophy mismatch.** Picking Monastic when you have a family, or Journalistic when your skill at on-demand focus is low. The mismatch fails and gets attributed to the practice, not the mismatch.
